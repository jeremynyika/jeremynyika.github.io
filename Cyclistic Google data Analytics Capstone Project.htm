Cyclistic: Google Data Analytics Project

For the capstone project to test the skills I acquired in the course, I used the Cyclistic Case Study. I chose this specific case study as it had the most interesting set of data which had the potential to involve calculations, format changes and appropriate question making which are all things I sought out when I enrolled for the course.

Summary of the Case Study:

Cyclistic is a bike-sharing company located in Chicago with over 692 stations across the state. I am assigned as a junior data analyst in the marketing department of the organization and tasked to find the trends amongst the different customer types that exist in the company; namely a casual customer type and a member customer type.

Process taking:

Step 1:

The first thing I did was to try and create questions that will be relevant in this situation before gathering the data. Asking questions allows me to know what kind of data I am looking for and why (giving context to the data).

The main question in this scenario is, How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently? - which allowed me to derive more sub-questions which are:

        What is the popular bicycle type used by each customer type?

        What are the popular days for the different customer types?

        What is their behaviour like during the day like?

After asking questions that are related to data, I also had to ask questions related to my analyses process, such as:

        Where am I going to retrieve and store this data?

        What software am I going to use to analyse the data?

        What visualising software am I going to use to visualize my findings?

I assessed my options and decided the most efficient and effective way to complete this task was to do it all in one software that I was familiar with. Therefore, the analysis of data and visualization of findings were all done in Excel (native Power Query for the cleaning process.)

Step 2:

The next thing I did was then to get the data from https://divvy-tripdata.s3.amazonaws.com/index.html and downloaded all the data for 2021 (from January to December). I then unzipped the folders and stored the files under one folder to make the merging easier.

 

Step 3:

I then Opened an excel workbook and merged all of the CSV file data into one table.

 

This table had over 6 Million records in total.

Step 4:

I then looked at the data to see if the data was dirty so I could clean it. I removed unnecessary columns and deleted records where the train station data (the starting location and final location) was not present. Not having the train station locations makes our data dirty because I would not be able to calculate the distance they travelled, in addition to this, records not having all the necessary fields would make the records incomplete.

I also removed duplicated records and also created new columns that would assist in finding insights from the data. I created a day of the week column, a starting time column, a month column and a duration column. The day column is to store the specific day of the trip, the starting time column helps show the starting hour they started to ride, the month hour shows the month the trip was done and the duration column shows the duration of the trip.

 

This data cleaning process resulted in over 2 million records being removed.

Step 4:

Now that my data was cleaned, I then stored this in a data model to store all the records because excel cannot store over 1 Million records. By doing this I was able to create Pivot tables in excel to find my insights.

From this we find out that the most popular days for riding for casuals were Friday, Saturday, and Sunday (the weekend) and for members, it was during the week. One possible explanation that could be made about the difference in the popular days for two types of customers is that those without memberships utilise the bikes for leisure purposes during the weekend and that members use these bikes as their main form of transportation to places such as work, grocery shops etc.

Conclusion:

From all of the findings and insights, the marketing manager can use this to find ways to make promotions for members and also find ways to change pricing for casuals during the day such as during peak hours prices are slightly higher and slightly lower during off-peak hours.
